Staff and Supporters

Learn about the people who helped make A New Leash on Life possible!

Our Founders

Carol Brown - DVM

Louis Nightengale - DVM

They were instrumental in raising the funds and building a state-of-the-art animal shelter in Ardmore, Oklahoma. Their tireless efforts to educate the public in the beneficial relationship animals can provide led them to helping form A New Leash on Life, Inc. We are forever grateful for their encouragement and support.

Our Staff

Barbara Lewis - CEO

She has owned and operated Listen Up Pup canine training center the past forty years, where classes are offered to puppies and adult dogs on basic manners, agility, tricks, Canine Good Citizen, and reactive dog class. Listen Up Pup also offers a free behavior hotline to the community. Prior to starting A New Leash on Life, Inc., Barbara Lewis was the Oklahoma Field Instructor for a national Service Dog training agency for sixteen years. Barbara is a certified member of National Association of Dog Obedience Instructors and a Professional Member of Association of Pet Dog Trainers.

Brenda Spink - Volunteer Coordinator

Brenda Spink serves as our Volunteer Coordinator, handling all inquiries and scheduling for Therapy Dog visits, and other activities of the volunteers. Brenda’s excellent organizational skills make her perfect for this job.

Geraldine Cummings - Puppy Raiser Coordinator

Geraldine Cummings enables future Service Dogs to be raised by volunteers until they are approximately fourteen months of age. During this time, Geraldine makes sure the volunteers have everything they need, and the pups are receiving necessary training. Having raised a Service Dog, Geraldine understands the needs of the volunteers.

Jonathan Cox - Contract Trainer

Jonathan Cox completed our three-year apprentice training program, teaches dog training classes at Pontotoc Technical Center in Ada, and offers private instruction, boarding, and training as the owner of Buckaroos Retrievers & Retreats in Paul’s Valley. His experience in the boarding kennel business is an asset to the dogs he handles for our agency, and he serves as one of the instructors in both our classes and in our prison program.

Shelia Ford - Contract Trainer

Shelia Ford began as a Puppy Raiser and completed our three-year apprentice training program. Shelia’s involvement with her children’s activities gives her the opportunity to put the final-phase training for Service Dogs to the test of many distractions.

Michelle Traw - Contract Trainer

Michelle Traw began training dogs for Therapy Dog work. Her background as a teacher for special needs children helped in the development of our Therapy Dog training program. She is also one of our prison dog trainers and a Service Dog Puppy Raiser.
